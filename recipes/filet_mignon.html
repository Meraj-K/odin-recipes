Filet Mignon

Description

Filet mignon is a high-quality cut of beef taken from the smaller end of the tenderloin, a muscle that runs along the spine of the cow. This particular muscle does minimal work, making the meat exceptionally tender, lean, and subtly flavored, qualities that contribute to its status as a luxury item on many menus.

The expense of filet mignon is due to several factors, including the limited quantity of this specific cut available on each cowâ€”usually only about 4 to 6 pounds of usable meat. Additionally, the overall demand for this tender, lean cut remains high, and it often requires more precise butchering skills, all of which contribute to its higher price point.

Ingredients

  • Filet Mignon Steak
  • Frying Pan/Black Iron Skillet
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive/Vegetable Oil
  • Butter
  • Rosemary & Thyme
  • Garlic and Onions(Optional)

Steps

  1. Turn on the stove get your pan hot
  2. Season filet mignon with salt and black pepper
  3. Add olive oil into the pan
  4. Once hot sear filet mignon (7-9 Minutes each side)
  5. Just before cooked take your rosemary & thyme with butter and base your filet for flavour
  6. Once done let your filet rest for 5-8 minutes so the filet doesn't dry out
  7. Finally plate your dish and enjoy!
